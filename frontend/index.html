Background

I’m Eliza Sparkes, a licensed independent clinical social worker dedicated to creating safe, affirming spaces for healing and growth. I use she/her pronouns and identify as white, mixed European background, queer, and cis-gendered.

Clinical Experience

I have over eight years of experience providing psychotherapy in community health settings with individuals, families, and groups. I've worked with people across the age and identity spectrum including immigrants, Spanish-speakers, LGBQIA+, trans and gender-expansive folks, and young adults.

Therapeutic Approach

My practice is deeply trauma-informed, client-centered, and relational. I'm guided by empathy, collaboration, and curiosity. I draw from extensive training in Narrative Therapy, EMDR, and parts work to help clients build insight, resilience, and connection.

Issues we can address

  1. Depression
  2. Anxiety
  3. Impacts of trauma
  4. Gender identity and sexuality
  5. Improving interpersonal relationships
  6. Family conflict
  7. White-identified folks confronting and unlearning racism
  8. Impacts of political environment

Working Together

I believe that most people come to therapy hoping to change something that isn’t working in their life and to alleviate suffering. That might mean processing past and current negative experiences towards healing, gaining deeper self knowledge, and increasing insight and resiliency. To that end, I believe that the client is the expert of their own life. My role is to offer curiosity, compassion, accountability, humor, education about trauma and other mental health challenges, and to provide a trusting, nonjudgmental space. I use an anti-oppressive lens. This means I’m always considering (and encouraging my clients to consider) the impacts that racism, sexism, capitalism, homophobia, and white supremacy culture have had on their life and what influence they have on people’s challenges. I see the therapeutic relationship as a space that might offer reparative experiences, and a “laboratory” of self-discovery and behavior change.
